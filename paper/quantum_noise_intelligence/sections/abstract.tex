\title{Physically-Bounded Zero-Noise Extrapolation with Information-Theoretic Model Selection for NISQ Error Mitigation}

\author{Quantum Noise Intelligence Collaboration}
\affiliation{Open-source project, \url{https://github.com/mapleleaflatte03/quantum-noise-optimizer}}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
Zero-noise extrapolation (ZNE) is a leading quantum error mitigation technique, yet standard implementations produce unphysical estimates and require manual model selection.
We extend physically-bounded ZNE with automatic model selection via the corrected Akaike Information Criterion (AICc), eliminating manual tuning while guaranteeing estimates within physical bounds.
Across 125 circuit-noise configurations, our method achieves 0\% unphysical prediction rate compared to 4\% for linear ZNE, with comparable mean absolute error (0.058).
We additionally present the first noise-focused Model Context Protocol server, enabling AI agents to autonomously perform quantum error mitigation.
\end{abstract}
