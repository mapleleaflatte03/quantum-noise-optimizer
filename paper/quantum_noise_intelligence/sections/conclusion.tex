\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented physically-bounded ZNE with AICc model selection, a method that guarantees physical validity of extrapolated estimates while automatically selecting the appropriate model complexity. Across 125 circuit-noise configurations, our approach eliminates all unphysical predictions (0\% vs.\ 4\% for linear ZNE) with no degradation in mean absolute error.

Our key insight is that information-theoretic model selection (AICc) is essential for ZNE in the small-sample regime: without it, complex models overfit and bounded optimization alone is insufficient. The combination of physical bounds and AICc provides a principled, fully automatic ZNE pipeline requiring no expert intervention.

Additionally, our MCP server implementation demonstrates that sophisticated quantum error mitigation can be made accessible to AI agents, opening a new paradigm for automated quantum computing workflows.

Future work includes: (i) hardware validation on superconducting processors when API access is restored, (ii) extension to multi-observable estimation with correlated bounds, (iii) integration of adaptive noise scale factor selection, and (iv) combination with dynamical decoupling and readout mitigation in a unified pipeline.
